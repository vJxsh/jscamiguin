About the Camiguin Boo Book

The Camiguin boo book, scientifically known as Ninox leventisi, is a rare owl species found only on the island of Camiguin.

Its name comes from its distinct “boo-book” call that echoes through forests at night. It typically lives in forested areas, especially around Mount Hibok-Hibok.

This owl plays an important role in the ecosystem by controlling insect and small animal populations. Being endemic, it is a vital part of the island’s biodiversity.

Conservation is important as habitat loss threatens its survival, making it a symbol of Camiguin’s unique wildlife.
