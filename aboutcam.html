The Island Born of Fire

Small in size, massive in wonders.

Location

Camiguin is a pear-shaped volcanic island in the northern tip of Mindanao. It is located in the Bohol Sea, about 10 kilometers north of Misamis Oriental.

It is the second-smallest province in the Philippines in terms of both population and land area, but it boasts the highest concentration of volcanoes per square kilometer in the world.

Short History

The island’s name is derived from the word "Kamagong", a species of ebony tree. Its modern landscape was literally forged by fire—the 1871 eruption of Mt. Vulcan submerged the old town of Catarman, creating the iconic Sunken Cemetery.

Today, it stands as a peaceful sanctuary that honors its volcanic past while welcoming the future of eco-tourism.

Known Attractions

From the crystal sandbars of White Island to the healing waters of Ardent Hot Springs and the majestic Katibawasan Falls.

Culture & Traditions

Famous for the Lanzones Festival celebrated every October, featuring vibrant street dancing that honors the island's bountiful harvest.

🏝️

Why Visit?

It offers a unique "triple-threat" of tourism: volcanic trekking, world-class diving, and serene island living all within a 64km circumference.

Camiguin Provincial Capitol

The Camiguin Provincial Capitol, located in Mambajao, serves as the official seat of the provincial government. It is where important government offices and administrative functions of the province are held.

The capitol building is surrounded by landscaped gardens and open spaces, making it a calm and scenic landmark in the capital town. It also reflects the province's commitment to governance, tourism, and development.

Visitors often pass by the area while exploring Mambajao, as it is near other attractions, restaurants, and coastal roads leading to White Island.
